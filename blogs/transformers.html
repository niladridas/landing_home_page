Neural Machine Translation

Neural Machine Translation (NMT) is an approach to machine translation that utilizes artificial neural networks to translate text from one language to another.

English Text:

"Machine learning is a subfield of artificial intelligence.."

French Translation:

"L'apprentissage automatique est un sous-domaine de l'intelligence artificielle."

NMT

The structural model for such NMT is a encoder-decoder pair that takes in a group of words in English and outputs a group of words in French that is the translation. This structure is made out parameters that needs to be learnt. If the French translation is the output \(\boldsymbol{y}\) and the English words are the input \(\boldsymbol{x}\), we define \(p(\boldsymbol{y}|\boldsymbol{x})\), determined by the encoder-decoder structure as function which when learnt, is maximum for a correct translation pair of English and French group of words. For a new group of English words that needs translation, we would then simply pass it through the encoder-decoder learnt structure to generate the French translation.

A potential issue with this encoder–decoder approach is that a neural network needs to be able to compress all the necessary information of a source sentence into a fixed-length vector. This may make it difficult for the neural network to cope with long sentences, especially those that are longer than the sentences in the training corpus. Cho et al. (2014b) showed that indeed the performance of a basic encoder–decoder deteriorates rapidly as the length of an input sentence increases.
